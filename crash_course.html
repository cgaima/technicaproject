VOTING 101

The general election takes place every four years on the first Tuesday of November.

The presidency is rewarded to the candidate that receives the most electoral votes.

What your vote does

When many head to the polls, they think that their vote will directly determine the next president of the United States. However, that is not true.
Your vote determines the number of electoral votes a candidate receives..
A.K.A
If one candidate receives more votes than the other, they receive all the delegates (electoral votes) from that state.

Why Your Vote Matters

Your popular vote goes towards the recepient of ALL the electoral votes in that particular state.

You may think that your vote can't make a difference because you only represent one vote, but many of people have this exact same mindset. If large amount of people have this mindset this, the decision is left in the hands of others who aren't garunteed to have the same opinion as yours.
